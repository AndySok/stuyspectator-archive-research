1. Rights and Responsibilities<br>&nbsp&nbsp&nbsp&nbsp&nbsp a. The Spectator will 
not publish any material for which there is evidence that the author -on or off the staff- is using 
the paper for that writer's own personal ends.<br>&nbsp&nbsp&nbsp&nbsp&nbsp b. The Spectator reserves 
the right to turn away any ads which it considers in poor taste under community standards.<br>&nbsp
&nbsp&nbsp&nbsp&nbsp c. Ads that appear in The Spectator are not necessarily endorsed by The Spectator.
<br>&nbsp&nbsp&nbsp&nbsp&nbsp d. As a general guidepost, The Spectator strives to cover every visitor
 to the school.<br><br>2. Content<br>&nbsp&nbsp&nbsp&nbsp&nbsp a. The Spectator will strive to print 
 fair, factual, accurate and informative content, at the same time offering entertaining and 
 interpretive material.<br>&nbsp&nbsp&nbsp&nbsp&nbsp b. The Spectator does not accept and/or publish
  everything it receives from an interested contributor. The editorial board will determine when such
   materials shall be printed.<br>&nbsp&nbsp&nbsp&nbsp&nbsp c. Any newspaper staffer may write 
   commentary; that writer's byline indicates that the opinions expressed are the writer's own. 
   Staff editorials are unsigned, and reflect the opinion of the managing board.<br>&nbsp&nbsp&nbsp
&nbsp&nbsp d. The Spectator will attempt to ascertain all possible story ideas for each edition, 
   but it will select story ideas for coverage based on standard values of news-worthiness.<br>&nbsp
&nbsp&nbsp&nbsp&nbsp e. The Spectator will not demean individuals because of age, race, religion, 
   sex, mental or physical handicaps.<br><br>3. Letters<br>&nbsp&nbsp&nbsp&nbsp&nbsp a. The Spectator 
   encourages letters to the editors so that readers might share in opportunities of a scholastic free
    press in an open forum.<br>&nbsp&nbsp&nbsp&nbsp&nbsp b. The Spectator will not print anonymous 
    letters to the editor.<br>&nbsp&nbsp&nbsp&nbsp&nbsp c. Letters will be chosen for publication on 
    the basis of timeliness, effectuality of writing and potential reader interest.<br>&nbsp&nbsp&nbsp
&nbsp&nbsp d. The Spectator reserves the right to edit letters for length, content, or grammatical 
    correctness. All letters must meet submission deadlines.<br><br>4. Corrections<br>&nbsp&nbsp&nbsp
&nbsp&nbsp a. If an error is printed that could affect the interpretation of the content by readers,
     it shall be corrected and proper facts placed in the following issue under headline "Corrections."
     <br>&nbsp&nbsp&nbsp&nbsp&nbsp b. The managing editor is responsible for corrections. It is 
     recommended that the "Corrections Box" be placed on the Editorial page, along with the masthead.
     <br>&nbsp&nbsp&nbsp&nbsp&nbsp c. All misidentifications and misspellings of names and titles will 
     be corrected.<br>&nbsp&nbsp&nbsp&nbsp&nbsp d. There is no unimportant fact! All factual errors
      must be corrected. The "Corrections Box" is a reflection of the integrity of the newspaper and 
      its desire to "get the job done right."<br><br>5. Prior Review Policy<br>&nbsp&nbsp&nbsp&nbsp
&nbsp a. The Spectator will not be reviewed by administration prior to its release to the public. 
      Any threats by the administration to withhold the newspaper from publication will not be 
      tolerated.<br>&nbsp&nbsp&nbsp&nbsp&nbsp b. A source who demands to read the reporter's completed 
      story before publication and perform editing tasks on the story will not be permitted to do so.
