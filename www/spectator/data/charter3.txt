<i>Staff</i><br>
The Spectator holds a staff recruitment each fall. The staff will be chosen by procedures determined by the Managing Board.<br>
It is extremely important for all member of The Spectator to attend meetings. At the beginning of every school year, the Managing Board will decide upon attendance requirements and other administrative procedures for that year.<br><br>

<i>Removal From Position</i><br>
The Managing Board can vote by a two-thirds majority to remove an editor from his or her position, and then can begin the process for selecting another editor. If the editor in question appeals to the faculty adviser, the faculty adviser can reverse the decision of the Managing Board. The faculty adviser may not remove any editor from his position, and may not select new editors.<br><br>

<i>Faculty Adviser</i><br>

A. Faculty advisors for The Spectator are chosen exclusively by the
editorial board.<br>

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;1. Candidates for faculty advisor are to be interviewed by members of
the editorial board, and the most qualified candidate, according to the criteria specified below, will be selected for the position of faculty advisor.<br>

B. Qualifications:<br>

1. Enthusiasm and commitment:<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;a. The candidate should have an active interest in journalism, even
if he or she does not have prior experience in journalism.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;b. The candidate must be willing to commit time and energy to The
Spectator.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;i. He or she must be willing to work with editors in the Spectator
office for as many hours and as many days as necessary during
production week, even past hours of pay.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;ii. He or she should work on The Spectator outside of school as well,
to work with editors or edit articles.<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;iii. He or she should be willing to work in the Spectator office on
weekends, if necessary.<br>

2. Positive Relations With the Administration:<br>
&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;a. The candidate for faculty advisor should have previously maintained
and must currently maintain a strong positive working relationship
with the school administration, especially with the Assistant
Principal of his or her respective department.<br>


<i>Appellate Procedures</i><br>
Any person who wishes to appeal any action or decision of The Spectator should make an appeal to the Managing Board. The Managing Board deals with all appeals or objections to decisions or actions of The Spectator.<br><br>

<i>Ratification and Amendment of the Charter</i><br>
Ratification of the charter requires an absolute simple majority vote of the Editorial Board. Amendment of the charter requires an absolute two-thirds majority vote of the Editorial Board.<br><br>

Signed by twenty-one members of the 1997-98 Spectator Editorial Board.
