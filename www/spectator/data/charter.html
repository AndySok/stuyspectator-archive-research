Duplicate of the Charter of The Spectator
Stuyvesant High School (Approved on May 18, 1998 by The Spectator Editorial Board)

The goal of The Spectator is to inform the Stuyvesant community (including students, teachers, administrators, and parents) of the significant events and issues pertaining to the school. The Spectator is committed to journalistically ethical practices in its reporting and writing.

The Spectator has been established as a forum for student expression and as a voice in the free and open discussion of issues. The Spectator provides a full opportunity for students to inquire, question and exchange ideas. Content should reflect all areas of student interest, including topics about which there may be dissent or controversy. Student journalists, in concert with a faculty adviser, will make the final content decisions for The Spectator.

All student journalists must recognize that with editorial control comes responsibility, including the responsibility to follow professional journalism standards. This charter establishes The Spectator's operating procedures.

Spectator Staff
Editor in Chief
The editor in chief is chosen by the outgoing editor in chief and approved by the Editorial Board. The editor in chief oversees all aspects of the paper and makes final decisions on all aspects of the paper, except removal from positions and the filling of positions.

Managing Editor
The managing editor is chosen by the outgoing and incoming editors in chief and approved by the Editorial Board. The managing editor oversees administrative aspects of the paper, coordinates departments, and reports directly to the editor in chief.

Department Editors
Each department has at least one editor, who is chosen by previous department editors and approved by the Managing Board.

Managing Board
The Managing Board consists of six members, including the editor in chief, managing editor, and four department editors who will be chosen by the editor in chief and managing editor. The Managing Board advises the editor in chief on all issues involving the newspaper.
In cases where the Managing Board votes, at least five members must be present, including the editor in chief. All votes are decided by a simple majority with the exception of removal (see "Removal from Position") The editor in chief decides in cases of a tie.
The Managing Board may vote to request the school for a new faculty adviser.

Editorial Board
The Editorial Board is comprised of all the department editors, along with the editor in chief and managing editor. The Editorial Board votes by an absolute simple majority, except for the amendment of this charter. The Editorial Board votes on approval of selections of editor in chief and managing editor, and ratification and amendment of this charter. Staff
The Spectator holds a staff recruitment each fall. The staff will be chosen by procedures determined by the Managing Board.
It is extremely important for all member of The Spectator to attend meetings. At the beginning of every school year, the Managing Board will decide upon attendance requirements and other administrative procedures for that year.

Removal From Position
The Managing Board can vote by a two-thirds majority to remove an editor from his or her position, and then can begin the process for selecting another editor. If the editor in question appeals to the faculty adviser, the faculty adviser can reverse the decision of the Managing Board. The faculty adviser may not remove any editor from his position, and may not select new editors.

Faculty Adviser
A. Faculty advisors for The Spectator are chosen exclusively by the editorial board.
     1. Candidates for faculty advisor are to be interviewed by members of the editorial board, and the most qualified candidate, according to the criteria specified below, will be selected for the position of faculty advisor.
B. Qualifications:
1. Enthusiasm and commitment:
     a. The candidate should have an active interest in journalism, even if he or she does not have prior experience in journalism.
     b. The candidate must be willing to commit time and energy to The Spectator.
          i. He or she must be willing to work with editors in the Spectator office for as many hours and as many days as necessary during production week, even past hours of pay.
          ii. He or she should work on The Spectator outside of school as well, to work with editors or edit articles.
          iii. He or she should be willing to work in the Spectator office on weekends, if necessary.
2. Positive Relations With the Administration:
     a. The candidate for faculty advisor should have previously maintained and must currently maintain a strong positive working relationship with the school administration, especially with the Assistant Principal of his or her respective department.
Appellate Procedures
Any person who wishes to appeal any action or decision of The Spectator should make an appeal to the Managing Board. The Managing Board deals with all appeals or objections to decisions or actions of The Spectator.

Ratification and Amendment of the Charter
Ratification of the charter requires an absolute simple majority vote of the Editorial Board. Amendment of the charter requires an absolute two-thirds majority vote of the Editorial Board.

Signed by twenty-one members of the 1997-98 Spectator Editorial Board. Seeking and reporting the truth in a truthful way

  • We will dedicate ourselves to reporting the news accurately, thoroughly and in context.
  • We will be honest in the way we gather, report and present news.
  • We will be persistent in the pursuit of the "whole story" and in the presentation of a fair and balanced article.
  • We will keep our word.
  • We will hold factual information in opinion columns and editorials to the same standards of accuracy as news stories.
  • We will seek to gain sufficient understanding of the communities, individuals and stories we cover to provide an informed account of activities.

Seeking the student body's interests

  • We will uphold First Amendment principles to serve the democratic process.
  • We will be vigilant watchdogs of the administration and institutions that affect the student body.
  • We will provide the news and information that students need to function as effective students.
  • We will seek solutions as well as expose problems and wrongdoing.
  • We will provide a public forum for the views of our diverse student population.
  • We will reflect and encourage understanding of the diverse segments of our community.
  • We will provide editorial and community leadership.
  • We will seek to promote understanding of complex issues.

Exercising fair play

  • We will treat people will dignity, respect and compassion.
  • We will correct errors promptly and prominently.
  • We will strive to represent all sides relevant to a story and not take sides in news coverage.
  • We will explain to readers our journalistic processes when possible.
  • We will give particular attention to fairness in relations with people unaccustomed to dealing with the press.
  • We will use unnamed sources as the sole basis for published information (background) only as a last resort and under specific procedures that best serve the public's right to know.
  • We will be accessible to readers.

Maintaining independence

  • We will remain free of outside interests, investments or business relationships that may compromise the credibility of our news report.
  • We will maintain an impartial, arm's length relationship with anyone seeking to influence the news.
  • We will avoid potential conflicts of interest and eliminate inappropriate influence on content.
  • We will be free of improper obligations to news sources, news makers and advertisers.
  • We will differentiate advertising from news.

Acting with integrity

  • We will act honorably and ethically in dealing with news sources, the public and our colleagues.
  • We will observe common standards of decency.
  • We will take responsibility for our decisions and consider the possible consequences of our actions.
  • We will be conscientious in observing these Principles.
  • We will always try to do the right thing.

Purpose
The Spectator, the official student newspaper of Stuyvesant High School, provides the community the opportunity for the exchange of viewpoints for students, staff, parents, alumni and other interested readers.

1. Rights and Responsibilities
      a. The Spectator will not publish any material for which there is evidence that the author -on or off the staff- is using the paper for that writer's own personal ends.
      b. The Spectator reserves the right to turn away any ads which it considers in poor taste under community standards.
       c. Ads that appear in The Spectator are not necessarily endorsed by The Spectator.
      d. As a general guidepost, The Spectator strives to cover every visitor to the school.

2. Content
      a. The Spectator will strive to print fair, factual, accurate and informative content, at the same time offering entertaining and interpretive material.
      b. The Spectator does not accept and/or publish everything it receives from an interested contributor. The editorial board will determine when such materials shall be printed.
      c. Any newspaper staffer may write commentary; that writer's byline indicates that the opinions expressed are the writer's own. Staff editorials are unsigned, and reflect the opinion of the managing board.
       d. The Spectator will attempt to ascertain all possible story ideas for each edition, but it will select story ideas for coverage based on standard values of news-worthiness.
       e. The Spectator will not demean individuals because of age, race, religion, sex, mental or physical handicaps.

3. Letters
      a. The Spectator encourages letters to the editors so that readers might share in opportunities of a scholastic free press in an open forum.
      b. The Spectator will not print anonymous letters to the editor.
      c. Letters will be chosen for publication on the basis of timeliness, effectuality of writing and potential reader interest.
       d. The Spectator reserves the right to edit letters for length, content, or grammatical correctness. All letters must meet submission deadlines.

4. Corrections
       a. If an error is printed that could affect the interpretation of the content by readers, it shall be corrected and proper facts placed in the following issue under headline "Corrections."
      b. The managing editor is responsible for corrections. It is recommended that the "Corrections Box" be placed on the Editorial page, along with the masthead.
      c. All misidentifications and misspellings of names and titles will be corrected.
      d. There is no unimportant fact! All factual errors must be corrected. The "Corrections Box" is a reflection of the integrity of the newspaper and its desire to "get the job done right."

5. Prior Review Policy
       a. The Spectator will not be reviewed by administration prior to its release to the public. Any threats by the administration to withhold the newspaper from publication will not be tolerated.
      b. A source who demands to read the reporter's completed story before publication and perform editing tasks on the story will not be permitted to do so. 6. Editors Policy
      a. The Editor in Chief and the Managing Editor of The Spectator cannot be members of any sports teams.
      b. Staff editors may be on sports teams, but should their behavior show evidence of an inability to balance both activities (missing meetings, failure to follow deadlines, poor quality of work, etc.), their position as editor will be reconsidered by the managing board, as per The Spectator Charter regulations.

7. Student Union Policy
      a. Editors of any staff of The Spectator may not hold any positions in the Student Union.
      b. Writers of any staff of The Spectator may hold a position in the Student Union but may not write or report on stories which deal with the Student Union.
      c. Editors may not be members of their respective Grade Advisory Councils.

8. Obituary Policy
      a. Stories involving the death of current students, administration and prominent or recent alumni will be covered by the news staff, avoiding all editorial comment. They will include a photo, when possible, and relevant facts concerning the death.

9. Conflict of Interest
      a. Students may not write stories on their own teachers or activities they may be involved in.
      b. A student who writes an article about a teacher he or she may have in the future does so at his or her own discretion. If a writer requests to be removed from a story for this reason, that request must be honored by his or her supervising editor.
       c. Spectator editors will not be mentioned in minor articles or in articles for which they have little importance. The same holds true for photographs.
      d. No athlete spotlights will be done on Spectator editors.

10. Crime and Punishment
       a. If a student or faculty member is accused of a crime, The Spectator will report on it based on standards set by professional newspapers.
      b. The names of all accused individuals over 18 years old will be included in the story, regardless of whether that individual is a teacher, student, administrator, etc.
      c. The name of any student under 18 years old who is being tried as an adult in the court system will be included in the story.
      d. A student under 18 years old who is being tried as a minor will have his or her name omitted from the story, even if that student wishes to include his or her name.
      e. Should a student be subjected to disciplinary action by the administration of Stuyvesant High School, The Spectator staff will decide on a case by case basis whether or not to include that student's name. The determination will be based on the severity of the offense, prominence of the student, severity of punishment and/or expectation of reader interest in the story.
       d. Names of alleged victims of crimes which do not carry a stigma (i.e. robbery) will be included and interviewed when possible.
      e. The names of alleged victims of crimes such as rape and sexual assault will be withheld.
